II. DISTRICT ATTORNEYS.

District attorney in each county, how elected or appointed.
His removal from office.
He is the public prosecutor in his county.
Must attend courts of oyer and terminer and sessions, and city courts, in his county.
If he fail to do so, substitute how appointed, and his compensation.
When to institute proceedings before magistrates, and attend before the magistrate and grand jury.
When to prosecute for penalties and forfeitures.
To deliver receipt for money received by him, and file duplicate.
His accounts, when and how filed.
Action against him for failure to pay over money.
Salary, how fixed and paid.
In what cases, he cannot act as attorney or counsel, in a civil action.
Must keep a register, and deliver it to his successor.
