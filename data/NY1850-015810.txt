V. LIEN ON THINGS DOING DAMAGE UPON REAL PROPERTY.

Lien on things doing damage may be enforced by distress.
Distress defined.
Fence viewers to receive notice in twenty-four hours.
Fence viewers to appraise damages.
Beasts distrained to be impounded.
Disposition of beasts when impounded.
Damage and fees to be paid when released.
Other property, and if there be no pound, beasts, when distrained, to be kept by the distrainer.
Notice and sale of property distrained.
When notice to be in a newspaper.
Notice to be served on the owner if known.
If damage be not paid, sale by an officer.
Manner of sale as on execution, and disposition of proceeds.
Security may be required before surplus ordered paid to the claimant.
