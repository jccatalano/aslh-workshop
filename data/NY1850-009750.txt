887. The attorney of a plaintifff, who resides out of the state, or is a foreign corporation, against whom costs are awarded to a defendant, is responsible to, such defendant thereforee, to an amount not exceeding two hundred dollars. And the attorney neglecting to pay the same, after due proceedings against him, may be removed by the court. But he may relieve himself from responsibility, at any time before judgment, by filing an undertaking, for the payment to the defendant of the costs and charges, executed by a sufficient surety.
