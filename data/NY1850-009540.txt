866. All statutes, establishing or regulating the costs or fees of attorneys and counsel in civil actions, and all existing rules and provisions of law, restricting or controlling the right of a party to a agree with an attorney, or counsel, for his compensation, are repealed; and hereafter the measure and mode of such compensation must be left to the agreement, express or implied, of the parties. But there may be allowed to the prevailing party, certain sums by way of indemnity, for his expenses in the action; which allowances are in this code termed costs.
