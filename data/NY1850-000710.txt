61. There must be a general session of the judges of the supreme court at the capitol in the city of Albany, on the first Monday of August, 1851, and on the first Monday of August in every second year thereafter, at which session the judges shall make such general rules as may be necessary, pursuant to section 186, and shall also appoint the times and places of holding the general and special terms and circuits for the succeeding two years, commencing on the first of January thereafter, and designating the judges to hold the same. The appointment so made, must be such, that for each general term there shall be designated at least one judge not elected in the district where the court is held.
