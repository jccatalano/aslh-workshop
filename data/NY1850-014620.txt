1343. Whenever it appears by satisfactory evidence that any person is held in illegal confinement, and that there is reason to believe, that he will be carried out of the state, or suffer irreparable injury before he can be relieved, by the issuing of a writ of deliverance, any court or judge, authorized to issue such writ, may issue a warrant, directed to any sheriff, constable or other person, reciting the facts, and commanding such officer or person to take the party and forthwith to bring him before the court or judge.
