After the jury shall have been duly sworn, they must proceed to view the property specified in the writ, and having heard the evidence offered, and duly considered the value of the property, they must assess the damages which the owner, or if there be several, which the respective owners, will sustain by being deprived thereof. They must make an inquisition signed by themselves, and by the sheriff, in which they must set forth the names of the several owners of the property, and the rights of each owner respectively, so far as the same can be ascertained by them, together with the amount to be paid thereforee by the people of this state, and to whom particularly; which inquisition the sheriff must forthwith return, together with the writ, to the supreme court.
