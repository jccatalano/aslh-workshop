1728. A judicial record of this state, or of the United States, may be proved by the production of the original, or by a copy thereof; certified by the clerk, or other person having the legal custody thereof. That of a sister state, may be proved by the attestation of the clerk, and the seal of the court annexed, if there be a clerk and seal, together with a certificate of the chief judge, or presiding magistrate, that the attestation is in due form.
