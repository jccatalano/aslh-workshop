251. A person is not competent to act as a juror, unless he be, 1. A citizen of the United States: 2. A white male inhabitant of the town or ward from which he is returned: 3. Over twenty-one and under sixty years of age: 4. In the possession of his natural faculties, and not decrepit. 5. Of good moral character, of sound judgment, and well informed: and, 6. Subject to assessment, for personal property belonging to him, or for land in his possession, which he holds under contract for the purchase thereof, upon which improvements have been made of the value of one hundred and fifty dollars; or having a freehold estate in the county, in his own right or in right of his wife, of the value of one hundred and fifty dollars.
