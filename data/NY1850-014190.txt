1301. Whenever the writ appears to have been duly executed, an order must be entered, declaring that the people of this state, on paying to the owner or into the supreme court, the amount assessed in the inquisition for damages, and also the costs and charges of the proceedings, shall be entitled to an absolute estate in such real property, as fully and effectually, as if the name had been conveyed to the people by the owner thereof; and immediately after the payment of such amount, the absolute title to such real property shall be vested in the people of this state.
