II. APPEALS TO THE COURT OF APPEALS.

Appeal in what cases taken.
Security must be given to pay damages, costs and charges.
If judgment for money, security to stay execution.
If to deliver documents, they must be deposited.
If to execute conveyance, it must be deposited.
If to deliver property, security for, also on mortgage sale.
Security given, proceedings stayed.
Respondent may enforce judgment in certain cases, on giving security, notwithstanding appeal.
Undertakings in one instrument or several.
Security to be approved, and to justify.
Perishable property may be sold.
