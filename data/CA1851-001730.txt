158. A motion to postpone a trial on the ground of the absence of I
evidence, shall only be made upon affidavit, showing the materiality of l
the evidence expected to be obtained, and that due diligence has been
used to procure it. The Court may also require the moving party to
state, upon affidavit, the evidence which he expects to obtain, and if the
adverse party thereupon admit that such evidence would be given, and
that it be considered as actually given on the trial, or otfered and overruled as improper, the trial shall not be postponed.
