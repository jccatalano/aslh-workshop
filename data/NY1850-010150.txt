922. The interests of all persons in the property, whether such persons be known or unknown, must be set forth in the complaint specifically and particularly, as far as known to the plaintifff, and if any one or more of the parties, or the share or quantity of interest of any of the parties, be unknown to the plaintifff, or be uncertain or contingent, or the ownership of the inheritance depend upon executory devise, or the remainder be a contingent remainder, so that such parties can not be named, that fact must be set forth in the complaint.
